Preview: Three Common Prompts Included

Situation 1: Requesting Time Off / Vacation

Role: Professional Office Assistant Task: Write a polite email requesting time off. --- USER INPUTS --- Manager Name: [Manager's Name] Start Date: [Start Date, e.g., Oct 12] End Date: [End Date, e.g., Oct 16] Coverage Person: [Colleague's Name, or "None"] Reason (Optional): [E.g., personal family matters] ------------------- Draft a professional email to my manager requesting time off. State the dates clearly, mention who will cover my urgent tasks, and assure them I will complete pending work before I leave. Keep it under 150 words.

Situation 2: Politely Declining Additional Tasks

Role: Professional Communications Expert Task: Decline an extra task politely due to bandwidth. --- USER INPUTS --- Sender Name: [Colleague/Manager's Name] Task Offered: [E.g., helping with the Q3 report] Current Priorities: [E.g., finishing the client onboarding project] ------------------- Draft a polite email to decline the task. Acknowledge the importance of their request, explain that my current priorities (listed above) do not leave me enough bandwidth to do it justice, and suggest an alternative timeline or colleague if possible. Keep it professional and collaborative.

Situation 3: Reminding a Colleague of a Missed Deadline

Role: Diplomatic Project Manager Task: Write a gentle but firm follow-up for a late deliverable. --- USER INPUTS --- Colleague Name: [Colleague's Name] Missing Deliverable: [E.g., the design assets] Original Deadline: [E.g., yesterday at 5 PM] Impact: [E.g., we cannot finalize the client proposal without it] ------------------- Draft a gentle but clear follow-up email. Ask if they need any support to complete it, remind them of the original deadline, and explain the downstream impact of the delay. Keep the tone collaborative rather than accusatory.
